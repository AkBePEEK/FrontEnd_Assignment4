Valorant

A free-to-play first-person tactical hero shooter developed and published by Riot Games. Valorant takes inspiration from the Counter-Strike series, borrowing several mechanics such as the buy menu, spray patterns, and inaccuracy while moving.

Modes

Unrated

Unrated (also known as Standard or Normals) is the base game mode in VALORANT. It is a Plant/Defuse mode for two teams of five players where each team plays as either Attacker or Defender.

Competitive

Competitive (also known as Ranked) is a game mode in VALORANT. It uses the same in-game rules and format as Unrated, but with a focus on higher-stakes competition.

Premier

Premier is a tournament game mode in VALORANT available only on PC. Matches use the same in-game rules as Competitive, but with a focus on top-tier competition and playing together as one squad throughout a tournament structure, including additional features taken from pro play such as map pick/ban phases and timeouts. At its highest level, it will serve as a direct connection from the playerbase to the professional scene.

Deathmatch

Deathmatch is a multiplayer free-for-all game mode designed to allow players to hone their gunplay mechanics or warm up before playing Competitive. Each player loads into a random map with a random agent that they own. Players start in a brief warm-up phase before the real game starts. Players cannot buy or use abilities but are automatically given Heavy Shields. They can buy whatever weapons they want.

Swiftplay

Swiftplay is a best-of-nine spike (Plant/Defuse) mode. It has most of the same gameplay rules as Unrated but with the following changes to adjust for its shorter duration:
All player start each half with two points for their ultimate.
No credit bonuses for loss streaks.
Credits earned based on round outcome have adjusted values and are the same for both teams, with the exception of round 2

Spike Rush

Spike Rush is a faster-paced game mode. Pre-round time is reduced to 20 seconds (usually 30) and game time is reduced to 80 seconds (usually 100). While the attackers have less time to plant, they also have the advantage of each player having a spike. The game ends when one team reaches 4 round wins, teams switch sides after 3 rounds.
